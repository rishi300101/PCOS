üå∏ What is PCOD?

Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD) is a hormonal condition in which the ovaries produce higher-than-normal amounts of male hormones (androgens). This hormonal imbalance can lead to irregular menstrual cycles, weight gain, acne, and difficulty in ovulation. PCOD is common among women of reproductive age and can be managed effectively with lifestyle changes and medical guidance.

‚ö†Ô∏è Causes and Risk Factors

  • Hormonal Imbalance: High levels of androgens (male hormones) and insulin.
  • Insulin Resistance: When the body's cells don't respond normally to insulin, the pancreas produces more, which can trigger the ovaries to produce more androgens.
  • Genetics: If your mother or sister has PCOD, you are at a higher risk.
  • Unhealthy diet and lack of physical activity
  • Lifestyle Factors: Poor diet, lack of physical activity, and high stress levels.
  • Inflammation: Low-grade inflammation in the body can stimulate polycystic ovaries to produce androgens.

üîé Early Symptoms to Watch For

  • Irregular Periods: Cycles that are unpredictable, very heavy, or missed entirely.
  • Acne and Oily Skin: Persistent breakouts, especially around the jawline.
  • Thinning Hair: Hair loss on the scalp that mimics male-pattern baldness.
  • Excessive Hair Growth (Hirsutism): Unwanted hair growth on the face, chest, or back.
  • Weight Gain: Difficulty losing weight, particularly around the midsection.

‚è≥ Long-Term Complications

  • Type 2 Diabetes: Due to prolonged insulin resistance.
  • Heart Disease: Higher risk of high blood pressure and "bad" cholesterol.
  • Infertility: Difficulty in conceiving due to irregular ovulation.
  • Endometrial Cancer: Thickening of the uterine lining from irregular periods can increase risk.
  • Mental Health Issues: Increased rates of anxiety, depression, and eating disorders.
